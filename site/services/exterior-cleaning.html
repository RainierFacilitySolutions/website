Window Cleaning

Glass, frames, and everything in between.

Dirty windows aren't just a cosmetic problem — buildup in tracks and frames can lead to discoloration, mildew odors, and even moisture damage over time. We clean the whole assembly, not just the panes.

  • Interior & exterior glass
  • Window frames & sills (dust, mildew, debris)
  • Tracks vacuumed and wiped
  • Hard-water and mineral deposit treatment
  • Skylights, atriums & storefronts

For high & hard-to-reach windows

Multi-story buildings, atriums, storefronts — handled safely with the right gear.

  • Water-fed pole systems for 2–4 story exteriors
  • Lift systems for taller commercial buildings
  • Industry-approved fall protection & safety protocols
  • Fully insured for elevated work

What pressure washing covers

  • Building exteriors & storefronts
  • Sidewalks, entries & loading docks
  • Parking lots & garage entrances
  • Dumpster pads & trash enclosures
  • Patios, plazas & outdoor seating
  • Concrete, brick, stucco, EIFS & metal panels
Façade & Pressure Washing

Restore your building's curb appeal.

Pacific Northwest weather is hard on building exteriors — moss, algae, road grime, and oxidation accumulate fast. We use the right pressure and the right detergents for each surface so your finishes come out clean, not damaged.

For larger properties we phase the work to avoid disrupting tenants, customers, or deliveries. After-hours and weekend service available.

Awning Cleaning

Don't let dirty awnings undercut a clean building.

Awnings are the first thing customers see — and the last thing most cleaning vendors think about. We safely clean fabric, vinyl, acrylic, and metal awnings using surface-appropriate methods that lift dirt, mildew, and bird soiling without damaging seams, stitching, or finishes.

Recommended frequency

Storefronts & retail: annually, or twice a year if near busy roads or trees.

Office buildings: annually with façade washing.

Restaurants: twice a year minimum — grease and food soiling builds quickly.
